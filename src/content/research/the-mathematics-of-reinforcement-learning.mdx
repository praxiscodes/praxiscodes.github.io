The Bellman operator explains almost everything in RL dynamics:

$$
(\mathcal{T}V)(s)=\max_a\left[r(s,a)+\gamma\sum_{s'}P(s'\mid s,a)V(s')\right]
$$

In tabular settings, $\mathcal{T}$ is a contraction under the sup norm, giving a unique fixed point $V^*$.

Approximation breaks this clean guarantee:

- Function classes may not contain the fixed point.
- Bootstrapping amplifies representation mismatch.
- Off-policy data shifts the effective operator.

Most practical stability heuristics are attempts to recover enough contraction behavior to keep learning coherent.
